AI & Machine Learning

Understanding the difference between AI & Machine

Understanding the difference between AI & Machine Learning Although artificial intelligence (AI) is a broad concept, there are subcategories under it.

Intelligent machines are generally referred to as AI. By giving computers data in the form of observations and real-world interactions, machine learning (ML) aims to make computers learn and behave like people while improving their learning over time on their own. A subfield of artificial intelligence (AI) is machine learning (ML).

Machine Learning is an integral part of artificial intelligence. It interprets and creates algorithms that help computers learn from the data they generate and do tasks without needing a programmer to write code.

Summary

The more general idea of machines' ability to perform tasks in a way that we would deem "smart" is known as artificial intelligence. One modern use of AI is machine learning, which is predicated on the notion that machines ought to be able to learn autonomously if given access to data.

Understanding Chatbots

Although any software that executes an automated activity is often referred to as a bot, we are specifically interested in the class of chatbots that operate within chat platforms. The simplest way to define a bot is as software that can converse with a person. For instance, a user may instruct the bot or pose a query, and the bot would react or take necessary action.

What makes a chatbot more desirable in the industry?

The core idea of a chatbot is the conversation. Although it has limited skills, a chatbot can and does have conversations with humans. That is not to suggest, however, that in certain limited circumstances, text or voice-based communication can be just as excellent as or even better than speaking with a real person.
